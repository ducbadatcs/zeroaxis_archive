This document was found in a book called "Unresolved Cases",
written by Louis Toler, a therapist who does research on dreams
and occult in his spare time.

Patient: Hi, I'm recently so tired. I kept dreaming of some strange land.

Louis: Welcome. Please take a seat. What's your name?

Patient: Daniel Maurice. Neighbor of that missing Charlote girl from 12 years ago. Recently she kept on appearing in my dreams.

Louis: Are you sure you are not obsessed with that girl in any way?
What does she look like, a 13-year-old girl or a grown woman?

Daniel: No. She's fully grown. She said she was indeed that girl, and I know because I was close friends with the Austanten family, and she had the little manners that only they have. My dreams usually starts with me and her sitting in an arbor in the middle of a giant garden with all different types of flowers everywhere. And I believe I saw that woman "Marie" that the news were talking about as well.

Louis: Why are you so terrified then?

Daniel: Because in every of those dreams, I always die. If you can name a way to die, I endured it in those dreams. I couldn't wake up. I could feel the pain but I couldn't wake up.

Louis: How long has it been since first you had that dream?

Daniel: 25 days.

Doctor, patient and priests found murdered, recalling 1930 murders

23-March-1942

On 21-March of 1942, signer Daniel Maurice and therapist Louis Toler along with several local pastors and priests were found dead, with a knife through their chest. Similar to the 1930s murder, there were no blood found in any of the victims.

When investing Dr Louis's office, officers found a mysterious note, that match Louis's handwriting, that only wrote: "Here lies the canvas". The meaning of the message is unknown, and it's suspected that the murderers are the same as the 1930 Austanten and Marcen murders. However, the truths remain unknown.
