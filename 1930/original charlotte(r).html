Disapperance of Charlotte Austanten

02-Sep-1930

On 9:00am Sunday, September 7, 1930, the family of Austanten wakes up for their Sunday church hearing. However, their head youngster, 13-year-old Charlotte Austanten, was nowhere to be found. She had blonde hair, blue eyes, and loves dolls. She's planned to be married next year to a youngster of the Marcen family. However, the Marcen family also didn't claim to have met her anywhere. Currently, the neighborhood and city of Harile urges residents to help. Please notice the police if the girl had been found, or if you have signs or evidence connecting to her.

Austanten and Marcen family, and police murdered alongside mysterious sighting of Charlotte Austanten

14-Sep-1930

On 14-Sep-1930, several witnesses reported that they saw currently-missing Charlotte Austanten walking on Blue River St, along with another woman identified as "Marie". Witnesses reported that they heard Charlotte and "Marie" spoke to each other, and that one keen-eyed observer even saw Charlotte's color became bright red. Attempts had been made to pursue Charlotte back to her family, but all failed.
It is reported later on that the 2 families: The Marcen and Austanten family, were murdered in the afternoon of the same day, by the same method: Every victim had a knife piercing through their chest. The department of security of Harile also met the same fate, being stabbed in the chest in mid-daylight, and the total nuber of victims were 25. What's strange about the case, was that despite being stabbed, there were no blood found in any of the victim's body. There were only one note left at the sight of the incident: "25? Charlotte likes that number! -Marie". Currently, police and authorities urge residents to find Marie and Charlotte as fast as possible.
